Inspired by Sam Ringer who got the idea from Patrick Collison, I started keeping track of the books I finished reading in November 2024. I've highlighted books in blue that I particularly enjoyed (though this is purely personal taste; I am not claiming that these books have literary merit) and books in green that I found so moving or impactful that I'd recommend them to almost everyone. Books marked with an asterisk (*) are ones I've read before (I like re-reading books; my mother likes to tease me by asking if I didn't understand them the first time around).

  • John Steinbeck: The Grapes of Wrath
  • William Gibson: Neuromancer
  • Ernest Cline: Ready Player One*
  • Johann Wolfgang von Goethe: Die Wahlverwandtschaften*
  • Rebecca Goldstein: Incompleteness: The Proof and Paradox of Kurt Gödel
  • Margaret Atwood: The Testaments
  • Wolfgang Borchert: Draußen vor der Tür*
  • Andy Weir: Project Hail Mary
  • Jennifer Ackerman: What an Owl knows
  • Plato: Apologie des Sokrates (Apology of Socrates)*
  • Margaret Atwood: The Handmaid's Tale
  • Franz Kafka: Der Process (The Trial)
  • Lance Fortnow: The Golden Ticket
  • Andy Weir: The Martian
  • Samantha Harvey: Orbital
  • Simon Sebag Montefiore: Jerusalem: The Biography
  • Friedrich Dürrenmatt: Der Besuch der alten Dame*
  • Han Kang: The Vegetarian
  • Robert Littell: The Amateur
  • F. Scott Fitzgerald: This Side of Paradise*
  • Friedrich Dürrenmatt: Die Physiker*
  • Steven Levy: Hackers: Heroes of the Computer Revolution
